What I do

I'm currently at IBM, tackling design challenges in the data visualization and analytics space, working with a diverse team of over a 100 people, made up of designers, researchers, engineers and PMs across 3 different locales.

Beyond the craft of design

I'm passionate about my craft, but I believe good design doesn't happen in a vacuum. It's more than the sum of the artefacts we generate as designers. (Be they user flows, wireframes, mockups or prototypes).

A design is evaluated by the quality of the experiences we end up delivering to our users. And the best way I've found to deliver on these experiences is by helping others on my team excel. One of the most challenging but rewarding aspects of my work is being a conduit between design, engineering and business strategy. Helping other designers improve their craft, and making design accessible to those new to it.

I think a lot about crafting effective design systems that work for both designers and FEDs. Personal and team process, and how to build and maintain a healthy team culture. Sometimes I write about it on Medium.

Other pursuits

When I'm not pushing code, pixels or sticky notes, I'm usually eating and drinking my way across the city one cocktail bar and ramen shop at a time. (Or failing comically at speaking the local language of whatever place I happen to be visiting at the time). Curious? Check out my instagram.
